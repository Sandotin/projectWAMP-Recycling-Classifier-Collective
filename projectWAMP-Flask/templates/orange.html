Recyclablity

If you have a yard, food scraps can be added to a compost pile outdoors along with other organic waste. You can make compost by following the given steps:

  • Combine brown and green items: Dried leaves, cardboard, newspaper are brown items that you can mix with plants and animals.
  • Water your pile: Sprinkle water on your pile but donâ€™t water it too much otherwise the microorganisms in the pile will become waterlogged.
  • Stir your pile: You should provide the pile with oxygen by turning it once a week with a garden fork. The best time to turn the compost is when the center of the pile feels warm.
  • Feed your Garden: When the compost no longer gives off heat and becomes dry, brown, and crumbly, it's fully cooked and ready for your garden.

Impact

By composting your food scraps, you can keep them out of landfills and allow them to enrich your soil and make more sustainable use of the scraps.
